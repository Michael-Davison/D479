Lodging

Taniti offers a wide variety of lodging options, from affordable hostels to luxurious four-star resorts. Visitors can choose from many small, family-owned hotels and an increasing number of bed and breakfasts. All lodging types are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government to ensure safety and quality.

Transportation

Most visitors arrive in Taniti by air, although some arrive on a small cruise ship that docks at Yellow Leaf Bay once a week. Taniti is served by a small airport that accommodates small jets and propeller planes. The airport is expanding to accommodate larger jets in the near future.

Ground Transportation

  • Public buses run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. in Taniti City, with private buses serving other parts of the island.
  • Taxis are available in Taniti City, and rental cars can be rented from agencies near the airport.
  • Bikes and helmets are available for rent (helmets are required by law).
  • Taniti City is flat and very walkable, and Merriton Landing is easy to explore on foot.
